Get a Roof Inspection — Not Just a Home Inspection

June 17, 2026 · 4 min read

Borrower Guides

Almost every buyer gets a general home inspection. Far fewer get a separate roof inspection — and that gap can be one of the most expensive mistakes in the whole process.

Why the general inspection isn't enough

A standard home inspector looks at the roof, but usually from the ground or the edge, briefly, as one of a hundred things on their list. They're generalists. A roof is one of the most expensive components of a house to replace, and problems up there — worn shingles, failing flashing, soft spots, hidden leaks — are easy to miss without someone who climbs up and looks closely.

The math is lopsided

A dedicated roof inspection typically runs in the low hundreds of dollars. A full roof replacement can run many thousands. Paying a few hundred to know exactly what you're buying — and how many years of life the roof has left — is some of the cheapest peace of mind in the entire transaction.

A roof inspection costs a few hundred dollars. A surprise roof costs many thousands. That's not a close call.

How to use it

If the roof inspection turns up problems, you have leverage: ask the seller to repair it, credit you for it, or adjust the price. Worst case, you walk in knowing what's coming instead of discovering it after the keys change hands. Either way, you bought certainty — and certainty is the whole point.
