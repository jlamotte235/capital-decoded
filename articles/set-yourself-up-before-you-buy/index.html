Set Yourself Up Before You Ever Make an Offer

June 20, 2026 · 6 min read

Borrower Guides

The best position in any home purchase is built before you start looking. By the time you're making offers, the advantages are mostly locked in. Here's the prep that separates confident buyers from stressed ones — split into the money side and the house side.

Get your finances in position

  • Tune up your credit early. Pull your reports, fix errors, and avoid new debt or big purchases in the months before you apply. A better score can mean a better rate, which is real money over the life of the loan.
  • Keep your finances boring. No new credit cards, no financing a car, no large unexplained deposits. Underwriters want stability; give it to them.
  • Get truly pre-approved (not just pre-qualified) so you know your real number and can move fast.
  • Budget for the full picture — down payment plus closing costs plus prepaids (taxes and insurance reserves). The last bucket is the one that surprises people.

Learn to read a house

  • Look past the staging. Cosmetics are cheap to fix; the expensive stuff is structural. Note the roof age, the electrical panel, plumbing, HVAC, and any signs of water.
  • Know the red flags that affect insurance — fire-zone location, old wiring, an aging roof. They can raise premiums or limit coverage.
  • Scout the basics that don't change — location, lot, noise, commute, schools. You can renovate a kitchen; you can't move the house.
Cosmetics are cheap and obvious. The expensive problems are quiet — the roof, the panel, the water stains. Train your eye on those.

Why it's leverage

A buyer who's financially clean and knows what to look for negotiates from strength: you can move quickly, spot problems early, and walk away calmly when a deal isn't right. Preparation doesn't just save money — it removes the panic. And calm buyers make better decisions.

Educational only — not financial advice. Your lender and inspector can tailor this to your situation.
